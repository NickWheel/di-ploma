RINA Systems Receives Second Notice of Allowance from U.S. Patent and Trademark Office.

RINA Systems is pleased to announce that we have received Notice of Allowance from U.S. Patent and Trademark Office for U.S. Patent regarding ENHANCEMENT OF SEARCH RESULTS, invented by Terence Carr and Leo Zamansky.

RINA Systems, LLC, a technology firm located in Cincinnati, OH focused on developing advanced cloud-based solutions for Big Data analysis and search today announced receiving a Notice of Allowance from the U.S. Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO) for U.S. Patent Application No. 16/428,220 related to certain systems and methods of proprietary continuous active machine learning search technology being marketed and sold as Rinalogy Search.

This additional patent provides broader coverage for Rinalogy’s proprietary technology which is intended to introduce a new and significantly improved method for search and classification. This innovation positions Rinalogy’s solutions as a disruptive force in the marketplace.

Rinalogy Search uses an iterative process giving users the ability to provide feedback on search results. The software calculates the likelihood of the results returned, against what a user is looking for. The new methods help users find what they are looking for, with speed and simplicity.

​Through its partnership with Sentio Software, LLC, RINA Systems is pleased to announce iCONECT’s integration of Continuous Active Learning with Adoption of Sentio Technology.

San Francisco, CA – iCONECT, a privately held 20-year industry leader in legal technology and makers of the award winning iCONECT-XERA document review platform, today announced the integration of patented Sentio artificial intelligence technology into iCONECT’s eDiscovery platform, to power its Continuous Active Learning (CAL). This integration is the first in a series of anticipated product developments since the recent announcement of Newfield Capital Partners as a strategic capital partner.

“We see AI everywhere from Spotify to Amazon to iTunes.” said CEO Ian Campbell. “By incorporating Sentio technology into the iCONECT platform, we’re creating a paradigm shift in how eDiscovery will be performed going forward.”
